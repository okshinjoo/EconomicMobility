## 2. To ASSIST

**Subject:** Data access question from a free student site

Hello,

My name is Shinjoo Kim. I run Empower (economicmobilityproject.org), a free
financial education site for first-generation and low-income students. Our
students section teaches community college students to check ASSIST before
registering so their courses actually transfer, and right now we simply
link them to assist.org for that.

I'm writing to ask whether there's an approved way for a site like ours to
access articulation data, such as an API, a data sharing agreement, or a
bulk export. The use would be small and clearly labeled: showing a student
whether the courses in their transfer plan appear in a current agreement,
with the academic year displayed, a link back to ASSIST as the official
source on every result, and refreshes on whatever schedule you require.

If nothing like that exists, no problem. We'll keep sending students to
assist.org, and I'd welcome any guidance on how you prefer sites to link to
it.

Thank you,

Shinjoo Kim
Founder, Empower
economicmobilityproject.org

## Org-specific middle paragraphs

**1. UNCF (uncf.org)** — Subject: Featuring UNCF scholarships on a free student site
Our scholarship finder is a small hand-verified list, and UNCF's awards
are exactly what our students should see. Would you be open to us listing
individual UNCF-administered scholarships in the finder, each credited to
UNCF and linked to its page on uncf.org? Students would always apply
through you.

**2. Scholarship America (scholarshipamerica.org)** — Subject: Listing your open programs on a free student site
You administer many of the corporate scholarships our students qualify
for, and we already feature the Dream Award. Would you be open to us
listing more of the programs you administer, each credited and linked to
its official page? If there's a list of programs you'd prefer surfaced (or
not), we'll follow it exactly.

**3. Hispanic Scholarship Fund (hsf.net)** — Subject: Deepening how we feature HSF
We already feature the HSF Scholarship in our verified finder and send
students to hsf.net to apply. If HSF runs additional named programs we
should list separately, or has partner materials for community sites,
we'd love to feature them properly.

**4. APIA Scholars (apiascholars.org)** — Subject: Featuring your full scholarship portfolio
We feature APIA Scholars in our finder today as a single entry. If you'd
prefer we list your individual scholarship programs separately so students
can see what fits them, we'd be glad to, each linked to your site.

**5. TheDream.US (thedream.us)** — Subject: Keeping our listings of your scholarships accurate
We feature both your National and Opportunity scholarships in our finder,
which has a filter for students without citizenship paperwork. I want to
make sure our listings stay accurate cycle to cycle. Is there someone we
can check with each year, or a partner update list we should join?

**6. Native Forward Scholars Fund (nativeforward.org)** — Subject: Featuring Native Forward scholarships
We'd like to add Native Forward's scholarships to our hand-verified
finder, credited and linked to your site, so Native students find you
directly. Is that alright, and is there a program list you'd like us to
work from?

**7. Ascend Educational Fund (ascendfundny.org)** — Subject: Featuring your scholarship for immigrant students
Our finder highlights awards open to students regardless of immigration
status. We'd like to list the Ascend scholarship with credit and a link to
your application page. (Note to self: Ascend is NYC-metro only; the listing
must say so plainly.)

**8. Point Foundation (pointfoundation.org)** — Subject: Listing your community college program too
We feature the Flagship scholarship today. We'd also like to list Point's
community college program, since a large share of our readers start at
community college. Anything we should know to describe it accurately?

**9. QuestBridge (questbridge.org)** — Subject: Materials for first-gen students on a free site
We feature the National College Match in our finder. Do you have outreach
materials for community organizations, or timing guidance you'd like
reflected, so our listing sends students to you at the right moment in
their junior/senior year?

**10. mikeroweWORKS Foundation (mikeroweworks.org)** — Subject: Featuring the Work Ethic Scholarship
Plenty of our readers are headed to the trades, not a four-year campus.
We'd like to list the Work Ethic Scholarship in our finder, credited and
linked to your application page. Is that alright with you?
